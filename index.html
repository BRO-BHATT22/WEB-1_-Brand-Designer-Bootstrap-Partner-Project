WAYNE SHORTER

WHO IS HE?

Wayne Shorter is an American jazz saxophonist, composer and bandleader. He rose to fame in the 1950s and 1960s as a member of Art Blakey's Jazz Messengers and Miles Davis' Second Great Quintet. Shorter has gone on to become one of the most influential jazz saxophonists of all time, winning 10 Grammy Awards and a Pulitzer Prize. His music is often characterized by its unique blend of jazz, blues, and classical music elements. Shorter has released over 20 albums as a leader, and has collaborated with many notable jazz musicians, including Herbie Hancock and Chick Corea. He continues to be a vital figure in the jazz world, both as a performer and composer.
